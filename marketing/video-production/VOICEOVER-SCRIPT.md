# "The Math" — Read This Into Your Phone

Record in a quiet room, speak naturally, confident but not rushing.
Pause where you see [...]. Let the words breathe.

Total read time: ~55-60 seconds.

---

In 1494, a Franciscan monk named Luca Pacioli published a book.
It described a system where every debit has a credit.
Every transaction balances. Every penny is accounted for.

[pause 1 second]

Five hundred and thirty-two years later,
we're building AI agents that can trade stocks,
manage portfolios, close contracts worth millions...

[stop walking. beat.]

...and not a single one of them uses Pacioli's system.

[pause. sit down. lean in.]

We built MnemoPay.
Double-entry bookkeeping for AI agents.
We stress-tested it with a thousand random transactions.
Fee plus net equals gross.
Every. Single. Time.

[slight smile]

But that's just the ledger.

[stand up. walk.]

We gave agents memory.
Not a key-value store... actual cognitive memory.
Backed by the same neuroscience that explains
how your brain remembers your first kiss
but forgets what you had for lunch.

[direct to camera]

Memory that decays when it should.
Strengthens when it matters.

[beat]

Six companies have raised eighty-seven million dollars
building pieces of this.
Memory here. Payments there.
None of them built both.

[lean in slightly]

We built all six layers.
On zero funding.

---

## Recording Tips

- Use Voice Memos (iPhone) or Recorder (Android)
- Hold phone 6-8 inches from face
- Quiet room, no echo (closet works great)
- Speak at 70% energy — confident, not shouting
- Don't rush the pauses. They're the most important part.
- Do 2-3 takes. Pick the one that feels most natural.
- Export/share as .mp3 or .m4a
