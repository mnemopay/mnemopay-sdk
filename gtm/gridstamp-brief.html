BVLOS Proof-of-Presence
Compliance Brief

For FAA Part 135 operators, BVLOS certificate holders, and their insurers

The audit gap: Telemetry logs prove a drone flew a route. They do not prove the data hasn't been altered. Any operator with storage access can modify them. Insurers and FAA auditors know this — which is why disputed claims and incident investigations increasingly require cryptographically verified, tamper-resistant evidence.

What GridStamp Does

GridStamp generates a cryptographic proof-of-presence at the moment of each delivery, waypoint, or inspection event. The proof is Merkle-tree anchored — meaning any post-hoc alteration of the location record breaks the hash chain and is immediately detectable. It runs on any drone compute hardware or ground station.

import { GridStamp } from 'gridstamp'; const gs = new GridStamp({ operatorId: 'your-part135-id' }); // Generates a tamper-proof stamp at delivery const proof = await gs.stamp({ lat: 37.7749, lng: -122.4194, action: 'delivery_confirmed', packageId: 'PKG-20260411-001' }); console.log(proof.merkleRoot); // Submit to insurer / FAA auditor

6-Layer Verification Architecture

Spatial Proof Tamper-evident location stamp. Holds up to adversarial audit. Ed25519-signed per event.
Anti-Spoofing Cross-checks GPS with accelerometer, time-of-flight, and behavioral baseline. Detects replay attacks and GPS drift.
Merkle Integrity Full flight audit trail chained as a Merkle tree. Any altered entry breaks the chain — detectable by any third party.
Identity Stability Cryptographic vehicle identity per session. Detects if the onboard system has been swapped or hijacked mid-flight.
Route Compliance Waypoint-by-waypoint verification against filed flight plan. Exports as FAA-compatible audit record.
Payment Settlement Optional: proof triggers automatic delivery payment settlement via MnemoPay integration.

Who Uses GridStamp

BVLOS operators under Part 135 (Wing, Zipline, Amazon, Matternet). Inspection drone operators (oil/gas, construction, utilities). Any operator whose insurer or enterprise customer requires third-party verifiable proof-of-delivery. AV fleet operators requiring route proof for liability.
