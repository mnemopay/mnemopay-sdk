## Abstract (paste into submission form)

As drone delivery scales from pilots to commercial operations, a compliance gap is widening: autonomous systems need to prove not just *where* they went, but *that the proof itself hasn't been tampered with*.

FAA Part 135 certificate holders, BVLOS operators, and their insurers all need the same thing — an auditable, court-admissible record that a vehicle was at a specific location, at a specific time, performing a specific action. Today that record either doesn't exist, lives in a proprietary black-box system the operator controls, or requires expensive third-party integration.

This talk introduces the spatial proof-of-presence stack: a six-layer cryptographic verification system that generates Merkle-tree-anchored location proofs in real time, deployable in 3 lines of code via the open-source GridStamp SDK.

Attendees will leave with:
- A framework for understanding where current telemetry falls short as legal evidence
- A concrete architecture for generating tamper-resistant proof-of-delivery, proof-of-inspection, and proof-of-route records
- Real-world performance benchmarks from field deployments
- A clear path to satisfying FAA, MSHA, EPA, and insurer audit requirements using open standards

This is infrastructure-layer work — not a product pitch. The full SDK is Apache 2.0.
