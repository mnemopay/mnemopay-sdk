**Feature logline:**
A high-powered Nigerian-American attorney in Dallas survives a drunk-driving accident and decides to walk from Texas to New York City to win back his wife — a journey that strips away fifteen years of performance and forces him to face his family, his failures, and who he actually is.

**POC short concept (under 15 minutes):**
The short captures a single night midway through the walk — Kolade in Memphis, broke and exhausted, sleeping in a shelter for the first time in his life. He calls his mother from a borrowed phone. The conversation that follows is the emotional core of the feature: two people who love each other deeply and have never once said anything true to each other. The short ends with Kolade walking back out into the dark, continuing north.

**Why this story centers Black characters:**
Eligible is entirely about the interior life of a Nigerian-American man — his specific relationship to immigrant expectation, Black masculinity, and the performance of success in white institutional spaces. This isn't a story adjacent to the Black experience. It is the Black experience, specifically and without apology.

**Project description:**
Kolade Adeyemi is a Nigerian-American attorney in Dallas. The night his marriage ends, he survives an accident he probably deserved — and decides to walk to New York City to get his wife back. Eligible is a single night on that walk: Kolade in a Memphis shelter, one borrowed phone call to his mother, and the conversation they've been avoiding for thirty years.

**Why this story:**
I'm Nigerian-American, Dallas-based, and I've watched the men around me become experts at performing success for everyone except themselves. Eligible is about what happens when the performance costs you the only person who was supposed to know the difference. I'm writing from close range.
