**Production Company:** Blackpig Films | Dallas, TX

**Genre:** Narrative Fiction Short

## Synopsis

Kolade Adeyemi is a Nigerian-American attorney in Dallas. He has the resume of a man who has won at life: partner-track, a beautiful wife, a mortgage in a good zip code. He also has thirty years of things he has never said to anyone.

The night his wife Amara announces she's done and leaves for New York City, Kolade gets drunk, gets behind the wheel, and survives the accident that follows. Lying on the side of a Texas highway, he makes the only decision that's ever felt completely his own: he's going to walk to New York City to get her back.

**The short film** picks up Kolade three weeks into that walk. He's in Memphis. He's broke, his suit is destroyed, and he hasn't slept in a shelter before tonight. He borrows a phone from the man in the next cot and calls his mother in Houston.

The call is ten minutes long. It covers thirty years. His mother has spent his entire adult life praying for a version of her son she recognizes. Kolade has spent his entire adult life becoming someone she doesn't. For the first time, neither of them pretends otherwise.

The short ends with Kolade handing back the phone, walking out of the shelter, and continuing north. He is not fixed. He is honest for the first time. That is enough.

---

## Style, Format, and Visual Approach

Eligible is a quiet film. Two cameras — one locked off on Kolade's face during the call, one hand-held observing the shelter around him. The contrast between his stillness and the ambient chaos of the shelter is the visual grammar of the film.

Color palette: sodium-vapor orange of shelter lighting, the blue-gray of early Memphis mornings. No score during the phone call — only ambient sound, breathing, the distant sound of the city. A simple acoustic theme plays under the final walk out.

The performance carries everything. This film lives or dies on the actor playing Kolade.

---

## How This Project Is Unique and Contributes to Society

American film has a long tradition of road movies — but almost none of them follow a Black man who chose to leave the highway. Eligible is about the margins of America that a man like Kolade has spent his career insulated from, and what it does to him to finally see them.

The contribution: a film that treats Black male vulnerability not as weakness or pathology but as the most human thing a person can do. The audience for this film — Nigerian-American families, Black men who have performed their way into exhaustion, anyone who has been the "successful one" in their family — is large and deeply underserved.

---

## Relationship to the Material

I am Nigerian-American. I grew up in a household that organized itself around achievement as survival strategy. I have watched men I love become strangers to themselves in pursuit of the version of success that keeps Nigerian immigrant families safe in America. I am not Kolade. But I know what it cost to stay on the highway.
