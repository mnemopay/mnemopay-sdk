I'm applying for the Short Film Grant with Eligible, a proof-of-concept short film that introduces the world of my feature film in development.

**Current status:** Pre-production. Script in development, key scenes complete. No crew formally attached yet — awaiting development funding to hire DP and editor.

**Script coverage:** Not yet submitted for coverage. Script pages included in the proposal.

**Potential investors/donors:** None committed. This is a bootstrapped production — I am a software engineer by day and filmmaker by conviction. Every dollar I put into Blackpig Films comes from my own pocket. Grants are the primary funding mechanism at this stage.

---

Eligible is a short film about a Nigerian-American attorney in Dallas who survives a car accident the night his marriage ends and decides — inexplicably, correctly — to walk to New York City.

The short captures a single night midway through that walk: Kolade in a Memphis shelter, broke and exhausted, calling his mother from a borrowed phone. What follows is a ten-minute conversation that contains thirty years of things neither of them has ever said. It ends with Kolade walking back out into the dark, continuing north.

I am writing from close range. I grew up watching Nigerian-American men of my father's generation and mine become very good at performing success — for employers, for institutions, for the version of America that rewards a certain kind of presentation. What I did not see was men who knew what they wanted when the performance ended. Eligible is about that moment.

The Roy W. Dean Grant's emphasis on films that are "unique and make a contribution to society" describes exactly what I'm trying to make. A film about a successful Black man choosing vulnerability over image is not a film that gets made often. I believe it is a film that is needed.

Thank you for your time and consideration.
