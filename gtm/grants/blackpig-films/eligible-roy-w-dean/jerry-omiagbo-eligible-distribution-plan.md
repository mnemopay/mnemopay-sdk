## Distribution Strategy

### Phase 1 — Festival Circuit (Months 1–12 post-production)

Primary targets:
- Sundance Film Festival — Short Film Program
- SXSW — Narrative Short Competition
- Tribeca Film Festival — Short Film Program
- American Black Film Festival (ABFF) — short film competition
- Pan African Film Festival (PAFF)
- Dallas International Film Festival (local premiere, press support)
- Atlanta Film Festival
- BlackStar Film Festival (specifically for Black filmmakers)

Goal: One major festival premiere to generate press, distributor attention, and credibility for the feature pitch.

### Phase 2 — Streaming (Month 12+)

**Primary target:** ARRAY Releasing (Ava DuVernay's distribution arm — ARRAY's mandate is films by and about people of color; Eligible is a direct match). Outreach to begin after first festival acceptance.

**Secondary targets:** MUBI (art-house short programming), Criterion Channel, YouTube Premium, Amazon Prime Video (short film licensing).

### Phase 3 — Community & Educational Screenings

Partnership with:
- Nigerian-American community organizations (Dallas, Houston, Atlanta, New York)
- HBCU film programs
- Immigration advocacy organizations
- Corporate DEI programming (law firms and financial institutions — Kolade's world)

**Why community screenings matter for Eligible:** The film's audience — Nigerian-American families, Black professionals, children of immigrants who have watched their parents disappear into performance — is a specific, passionate, underserved audience that festival distribution alone doesn't reach. Direct community screenings are the film's impact strategy.

## Social Media Plan

**Platform:** Instagram and TikTok under @blackpigfilms

**Content strategy:**
- Behind-the-scenes production documentation
- Director's journal entries during pre-production and shoot
- Short-form video essays about the themes of Eligible (diaspora, Black masculinity, road films)
- Teaser/trailer campaign building to festival premiere

**Target audience online:** Nigerian-American diaspora community, Black film enthusiasts, independent film followers, social justice-oriented film audiences.

**Estimated organic reach before premiere:** 2,000–5,000 followers (realistic for a bootstrapped indie film with authentic community content).

---

## Long-Term Vision

Eligible the short film is the proof of concept for Eligible the feature. Every festival placement, every community screening, every press mention is evidence of audience demand for the feature. The short is designed to open the door — to financiers, to agents, to Sundance's feature labs, to the ARRAY feature fund.

The goal is not to make a short film. The goal is to make the feature. The short is how we prove it deserves to exist.
