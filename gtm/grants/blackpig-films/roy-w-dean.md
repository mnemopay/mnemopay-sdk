Eligible follows Kolade, a high-powered Nigerian-American attorney in Dallas who has spent fifteen years building exactly the life his immigrant parents imagined for him — and none of the life he actually wanted. After a catastrophic fight with his wife, who packs up and moves back to New York City, Kolade gets drunk, gets behind the wheel, and survives an accident that should have killed him. What happens next is the movie: he decides to walk to New York City to get her back.

This story comes directly from my own experience navigating two worlds — the expectations of a Nigerian household and the realities of building a life in America. I've watched people I love become strangers to themselves trying to hold both at once. Eligible is about what happens when the performance finally cracks.

## SYNOPSIS (1 page)

**ELIGIBLE**
Written & Directed by Jerry Omiagbo | Blackpig Films | Dallas, TX

Kolade Adeyemi has the resume of a man who has won at life. Partner-track at a Dallas law firm, a beautiful wife, a mortgage in a good zip code. What he doesn't have — and has never allowed himself to want — is any of it on his own terms.

The night his wife Amara announces she's done and moves back to New York City, Kolade does what men like him do: he goes out, drinks too much, and gets in a car. The accident that follows should be the end of everything. Instead, lying on the side of a Texas highway with a totaled car and a mostly intact body, he makes the only decision that's ever felt completely his: he's going to walk to New York City and get his wife back.

What follows is a journey across America that dismantles Kolade piece by piece and puts him back together differently. Walking strips away the armor of a man who has never had to be without it. He sleeps in shelters, gets robbed in Memphis, is hospitalized in Virginia with no ID and no money. Along the way he calls his mother, who has spent thirty years praying for a son she recognizes, and finally meets his estranged brother, who never learned the performance Kolade perfected. Every mile is a negotiation between who he was trained to be and who he might actually be.

By the time Kolade reaches New York City, the question is no longer whether Amara will take him back. The question is whether the man who arrives is someone she — or he — has ever met before.

**Eligible** is a road film and a marriage film and a film about what it costs to be the successful one. It is specific to the Nigerian-American experience and universal in the way that only very specific stories can be.

## DIRECTOR'S STATEMENT

My father came to America with two suitcases and the absolute conviction that education was the mechanism by which Black men from Nigeria could be treated like humans in this country. He was right. He was also wrong.

I grew up watching the men around me become very good at being legible — to employers, to institutions, to the version of America that rewards a certain kind of performance. What I didn't see much of was men who knew what they actually wanted outside of that performance. What happened when the performance ended.

Kolade is not autobiographical. But the wound he carries is one I recognize from every dinner table and every church pew I've ever sat at with Nigerian men of a certain generation and education level. The walk is my way of putting that wound on a highway with nowhere to hide.

Visually, I want Eligible to feel like America looks when you're not in a car — wide and indifferent and occasionally beautiful in ways that have nothing to do with you. Long lenses, ambient sound, the texture of roadside America that nobody photographs. Kolade in a suit that gets progressively more destroyed. The walk as a physical record of transformation.

## DISTRIBUTION PLAN

**Phase 1 — Festival Circuit (Year 1)**
Submit to Sundance, SXSW, Tribeca, Los Angeles Film Festival, and the African American Film Critics Association (AAFCA) showcase. Target: one major festival premiere, building press and distribution interest.

**Phase 2 — Streaming (Year 2)**
Primary target: Netflix, Amazon Prime, or ARRAY Releasing (Ava DuVernay's distribution arm focused on films by and about people of color — direct alignment). Secondary: MUBI, Criterion Channel.

**Phase 3 — Community Screenings**
Partner with Nigerian-American community organizations, HBCUs, and immigration advocacy groups for targeted screenings. Eligible's themes of diaspora identity, marriage, and family have built-in community audiences who are underserved by mainstream distribution.

**Social media:** Blackpig Films Instagram/TikTok documenting the production process, building audience before release.
