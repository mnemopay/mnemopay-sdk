## Business description (250 words max)

J&B Enterprise LLC is a one-person Texas LLC building AI-native software infrastructure out of Dallas. The core product, MnemoPay, is an open-source payment and reputation SDK for AI agents — the plumbing that lets autonomous software handle real transactions without a human in the loop. It's live on npm, used by developers building AI agent systems, and has 700+ tests and a working payment layer across Stripe, Lightning, and Paystack.

The second product, GridStamp, is spatial evidence software for delivery robots and autonomous vehicles — it generates cryptographic receipts proving where a robot was at a specific moment, designed for insurance claims and incident investigations.

BizSuite is the revenue wedge: a $997 AI automation toolkit for small businesses that replaces $20K-$35K/year of disconnected SaaS subscriptions. It currently drives the company's primary revenue alongside consulting engagements.

All three products are bootstrapped, built without outside capital, and open source under Apache 2.0.

## Community impact statement (250 words max)

J&B Enterprise LLC is owned and operated by Jerry Omiagbo, a Black founder based in Dallas-Fort Worth. The community impact runs in three directions.

First, the products directly serve underrepresented markets. MnemoPay's Paystack integration was built specifically for African fintech ecosystems — giving developers in Nigeria, Ghana, and Kenya access to agent-payment infrastructure that the US fintech world has had for years. Dele, a ride-hailing and delivery app also under the J&B umbrella, serves Nigerian users directly and keeps transaction revenue inside local economies instead of routing it through Western payment networks.

Second, the open-source strategy puts production-grade AI infrastructure in the hands of developers who can't afford it otherwise. Every module — identity, ledger, fraud detection, reputation scoring — is free to use and modify under Apache 2.0.

Third, growth creates Dallas jobs. The first two hires will be a local customer success contractor and a part-time content producer — not a remote team, not outsourced labor.

The $5,000 grant would fund a single paid acquisition pilot for BizSuite — enough to run a statistically meaningful Meta/LinkedIn test and find the repeatable customer acquisition cost. That one data point unlocks the next stage of growth, including the Dallas-area hires.
